Supernova is the first major outdoor festival of digital animation and art, initiated by Denver Digerati, Arts and Venues Denver, and the Denver Theatre District in 2016. The festival utilizes a robust network Denver Digerati has developed over five years through an ongoing series of “Friday Flash programs” that have brought advances in digital animation and motion art to a network of public LED screens situated in Denver’s downtown core as a new public art viewing experience. The program promotes the dynamic, rapidly advancing impact technology is having in art throughout the world today. Denver is the only city in the world to support a sustainable protocol that presents digital animation as public art in a meaningful and ongoing context via outdoor LED Screens.

CORE FESTIVAL ELEMENTS

Seven hours of continuous film screenings of short-form digital content on 2 well situated LED screens, located at corners of 14th and Champa and 14th and Arapahoe in downtown Denver. Marketed as the first ever downtown, outdoor digital animation and art festival, presenting Curated artworks and programs selected through an international call for entries.

S1-2 hour Competition Screening on inflateable screen situated within Denver Performing Art Center (DPAC) common area, and cash prize awards selected by three distinguished guest jurors chosen for their prestige within digital media on an international level.

Interactive new media artwork installed adjacent to DPAC screening showcase, from local artist, group, or other depending on sponsorship funding or artist interest.

Facilitate limited “BYOB (Bring Your Own Beamer)” activations along 14th street corridor between Champa and Arapahoe Street and within DPAC corridor.

Live new-media music performance and visuals to follow the competition screening leading up to after-party. Thug Entrancer (Ryan McRyhew and Milton Croissant III) TBD.

Educational forum featuring Juror presentations, professional critiques, panel discussions and small-screen art engagement throughout day at Commons on Champa innovation center. Limited attendance, ticketed event. Possible 2 and 3d art exhibition at the facility for up to three weeks preceeding the eduction forum.

Additional LED screen limited content activations at the 15th / 16th and Champa Screens through Branded Cities, and all DPAC screens through AVD, with 10-20 second works, continuous throughout the evening.

Snap-shot “awereness” screenings in both public and private venues around Denver leading up to the festival TBD.
