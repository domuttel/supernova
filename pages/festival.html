Supernova is the first major outdoor festival of digital animation and art, initiated by Denver Digerati, Arts and Venues Denver, and the Denver Theatre District in 2016. The festival utilizes a robust network Denver Digerati has developed over five years through an ongoing series of “Friday Flash programs” that have brought advances in digital animation and motion art to a network of public LED screens situated in Denver’s downtown core as a new public art viewing experience. The program promotes the dynamic, rapidly advancing impact technology is having in art throughout the world today. Denver is the only city in the world to support a sustainable protocol that presents digital animation as public art in a meaningful and ongoing context via outdoor LED Screens.

CORE FESTIVAL ELEMENTS

Seven hours of continuous screenings of diverse and dynamic short-form digital content on our most prominent LED screen, located at the corner of 14th and Champa, with additional content running concurrently on a second LED screen at 14th and Arapahoe, both in the heart of downtown Denver.

90 minute Competition Screening on both public LED screens as well as an inflateable screen situated within the Galleria at Denver Performing Arts Complex (DPAC). Cash prize awards to be selected by three distinguished guest jurors chosen for their prestige within digital media on an international level.

Live new-media music performance by Thug Entrancer (Ryan McRyhew and Milton Croissant III) to follow the competition screening, leading into the evening’s after-party.

Education forum featuring Juror presentations, professional critiques, panel discussions and small-screen art engagement throughout day at Commons on Champa innovation center. Limited attendance, ticketed event.

Snap-shot “awereness” screenings in both public and private venues around Denver leading up to the festival TBD.

VIP Preview and affiliated pop-up show on Friday evening, September 23rd taking place at Superordinary, located inside the Source on Brighton Bouleverard.
