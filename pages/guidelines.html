GUIDELINES

Supernova will accept submissions from anywhere in the universe. All submissions should be suitable for display in a public context, please review Denver Digerati history for suitability purposes.

Supernova is only accepting works built primarily through digital means and techniques, please no “live-action” works unless they integrate appropriately with a digital interface. See categories on entry page.

All submissions should be between 60 seconds to 10 minutes in length, except Gifs. If you feel strongly enough that your work should be considered outside these parameters, you may still submit.

All submissions must have been completed no earlier than 2015. Priority will be given to new works that are not yet available for viewing online until after the festival takes place. Supernova will consider “works in progress” that are not fully complete before the deadline.

Supernova LED screens have two frame ratios: 1462 pixels high x 1100 pixels wide (14th and Champa Main Screen) and 360 pixels high x 456 pixels wide (14th and Arapahoe Screen). DPAC competition screen will accept all formats, but please note which screen will fit your work the best. Festival entries will be formatted with black masking as necessary.

Works may be submitted as digital files in .mov or mp4 formats at highest quality of resolution available. Vimeo Links also accepted. Gif’s accepted as website links, via dropbox or other common download systems.

Artists who have previously exhibited with Denver Digerati may enter competition at no fee. Artists who have previously been commissioned by Denver Digerati may enter at no fee, but are not eligible for awards in the competition section.

All artists may submit up to two works for consideration.

Early entry deadline deadline: June 15th, 2016. Works entered by early deadline may be sampled up to 20 seconds during July and August 2016 “Friday Flash” programs on 14th and Champa LED screen.

Final deadline for all entries: August 15th, 2016.
